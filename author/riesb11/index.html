  • Oily vs Dry Skin: What’s Your Skin Type?

    Understanding your skin type helps you choose the right products.

    Oily Skin

    Image source: McLean Dermatology – “Does Oily Skin Age Better?”

    • Shiny appearance
    • Large pores
    • Acne-prone

    Use: lightweight, oil-free products

    Dry Skin

    Image source: AFC Urgent Care – dry skin article

    • Flaky texture
    • Tight feeling
    • Dull look

    Use: hydrating, cream-based products

    How to Test

    Wash your face and wait 1 hour:

    • Shiny → oily
    • Tight → dry

  • Best Skincare Routine for Beginners

    Starting a skincare routine doesn’t have to be complicated. In fact, the best routines are simple, consistent, and tailored to your skin type. Whether you’re dealing with acne, dryness, or just want clearer skin, building a basic routine is the first step to healthy, glowing skin.

    Image source: Good Housekeeping – “Best Skincare Products” article

    Morning Routine

    Your morning routine is all about protection and hydration.

    1. Cleanser

    Use a gentle cleanser to remove oil and sweat that builds up overnight. This helps keep your pores clean and prevents breakouts.

    2. Moisturizer

    Moisturizing keeps your skin hydrated and prevents dryness throughout the day. Even oily skin needs moisture to stay balanced.

    3. Sunscreen (SPF 30+)

    This is the most important step. Sunscreen protects your skin from UV damage, premature aging, and dark spots.

    Never skip sunscreen — even on cloudy days.

    Images sourced from Unsplash

    Night Routine

    Your night routine focuses on repair and recovery.

    1. Cleanser

    Wash away dirt, oil, and any makeup from the day. Clean skin allows your products to work better.

    2. Treatment (Optional)

    If you have acne or specific concerns, this is where you can use a treatment like a serum.

    3. Moisturizer

    At night, your skin repairs itself. A good moisturizer helps support this process and keeps your skin soft.

    Choosing the Right Products

    Not all products work for everyone. Choosing based on your skin type is key:

    Sensitive skin: fragrance-free and gentle formulas

    Oily skin: lightweight, oil-free products

    Dry skin: thicker, cream-based products

    Images sourced from Freepik

    Avoid these mistakes

    • Using too many products at once
    • Skipping sunscreen
    • Changing products too often
    • Over-washing your face

    Final Tips for Beginners

    • Keep your routine simple (3–4 steps is enough)
    • Be consistent — results take time
    • Introduce new products slowly
    • Listen to your skin

    Great skin doesn’t happen overnight, but a simple routine done daily will make a big difference.
